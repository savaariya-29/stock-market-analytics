What is a Stock?

A stock (also known as equity) represents a share in the ownership of a company. Buying a stock means you own a small fraction of that corporation. Stocks are bought and sold on stock exchanges, and their prices fluctuate based on supply and demand, company operational performance, economic trends, and market sentiment.

How Does it Work?

Companies issue stocks to raise capital for their business operations. Investors buy these stocks with the expectation that the company will perform well, which can lead to the stock's price appreciating over time or the company paying out dividends.
